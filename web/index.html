Synthesizing the Roughness of Textured Surfaces
for an Encountered-type Haptic Display
using Spatiotemporal Encoding

Yaesol Kim, Siyeon Kim, Uran Oh, and Young J. Kim

 Relevant Work  PAPER

INTRODUCTION


We propose a novel yet simple, texture synthesizing method for an encountered-type haptic display using spatial and temporal encoding of roughness, which can provide both active and passive touch sensation requiring no complicated tactile actuation. Focused on macro-scale roughness perception, we geometrically model the textured surface with a grid of elliptical bumps, which can provide a variety of perceived roughness as the user explores the surface with one's bare hand. Our texture synthesis method is based on two important hypotheses. First, we assume that perceptual roughness can be spatially encoded along the radial direction of a textured surface with elliptical bumps. Second, perceptual roughness temporally varies with the relative velocity of a scanning human hand with respect to the surface. To validate these hypotheses on our spatiotemporal encoding method, we have implemented an encountered-type haptic texture rendering system using an off-the-shelf collaborative robot that can also track the user's hand using IR sensors. Our empirical experiments show that the users perceive a more rough texture as the surface orientation or the relative hand motion increases. Based on these findings, we show that our visuo-haptic system can synthesize an appropriate level of roughness corresponding to diverse visual textures by suitably choosing encoding values.

OUR APPROACH

TEXTURED SURFACE MODELING

  • We encode the tactile roughness by arranging an embossed dot pattern on a flat surface.

  • Our two modeling parameters are dot width and the dot spacing that can be moulated in runtime.


    • Perceptual width (wθ) of texton
  • We assume that as wθ of a texton along a scanning direction increases,
    the perceived roughness increases.

  • Elliptically-shaped texton was selected to control the texton width in runtime.

  • The encoded roughness increases with increasing θ.
    • Inter-distance (dθ) of textons
  • Textons should be arranged in such a geometric way that distance between tactile elements decrease as θ increase.

  • As dθ decreases, the perceived roughness increases.

  • We arragned the textons in a rectangular grid so that the encoded roughness increases with increasing θ.

    • OUR APPROACH

    HAPTIC TEXTURE RENDERING

  • A textured surface is attached to the end-effector of the haptic device and interacts with the user's bare hand.

  • The end-effector rotates a textured surface model to set orientation relative to the user's hand scanning direction.

  • The end-effector translates a textured surface model to provide a proper relative velocity to the user's hand.

  • Encoded roughness of virtual objects are rendered to the ueser and make the user feel different roughness of textures.

    • SYSTEM

    SYSTEM SETUP

    • KUKA LBR IIWA 7 R800 is used as an encountered-type haptic manipulator.

    • A 3D printed Textured Surface Model is attached to the IIWA end-effector and serves as a proxy for virtual obejct while synthesizing texture roughness.

    • Leap Motion is used as a motion sensor to track the user's hand.

    • Oculus Rift CV1 is used to visually render 3D virtual scene and track the user's head.

    SYSTEM

    ENCOUNTERED-TYPE HAPTIC TEXTURING SYSTEM

    User Tracking
  • Head and hand positions of user are tracked by HMD and IR Sensor.
  • Tracked data are transformed from the sensor frame to the robot base frame.
  • Midpoint Smoothign algorithm was used for reducing tracking noise and generating a smooth robot motion.


    • Contact Prediction and Detection
  • Contact is predicted in VR environment as the hand approacheds an object.
  • Pysical Contact is detected in real world using joint torque sensors embedded in the robot.


    • Haptic Rendering
  • A 7-DoF manipulator, capable of human-robot-coexistence, operates as a haptic device.
  • Textured surface model is attached to the end-effector of the manipulator and used as a proxy for a virtual object.
  • Kinesthetic feedback is provided by position-based control and force-based control.
  • Tactile feedback is provided by adjusting the orientation and velocity of the textured surface model.


    • Visual Rendering
  • A user's virtual hand and a virtual environment are rendered through the HMD.

    • USER STUDY

    PRELIMINARY STUDY

  • Examine if altering users’ roughness perception by changing scanning direction of our model is feasible.

  • Decide on the surface design of our model for simulating a range of perceived roughness as wide as possible.


    •

    Apparatus

    Four surface textured models with various raddi in x axis (3DS MAX and Form2 3D printer was used)



    Five different scanning directions parameterized by angles

    Experimental Setup

    Subjects were asked to graze each of the 4 patches in horizontal direction under 5 different orientations
    and evaluate roughness using a number while each patch was covered by an opaque cloth.

    Results

  • Both radii (F(3) =20.4, p<.001) and orientation (F(4) =17.2, p<.001) have signiﬁcant main effects.

  • It is feasible to change perceived roughness by varying the scanning direction using our model.

  • We chose a textured surface model with rx = 3.0mm for our encountered-type haptic system which showed the widest range of average perceived roughness to maximize the effect.
